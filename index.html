The Sunday Ritual

A morning designed
to stay with you.

Every Sunday. Same rhythm. Changing only by season, city, and the women who join.

7:15 am

Arrive

A quiet corner of the city. You find us by a landmark, not a busy entrance. Small group already gathering. Someone hands you a coffee order slip.

7:30 am

We Walk

No phones out. No headphones. A 45–60 minute route through the neighbourhood — scenic, unhurried, and always a little unexpected.

8:30 am

Coffee Together

We settle into a local café with good light and real tables. Introductions happen naturally. Conversations run long. Nobody checks the time.

10:00 am

Until Next Sunday

That's it. No agenda. No commitment beyond showing up. But somehow, you already feel like coming back.

Our Story

Built slowly.
On purpose.

How it started.

It started with one Sunday, one park, and four women who all said the same thing afterwards: "We should do this again."

No apps. No group chats with 200 strangers. No performance. Just a walk, a table at a café, and the slow realisation that this was something we'd all been missing.

Girls Who Walk grew from that one morning. It remains small because that's how it works best. The intimacy is the point.

Our Values

What we believe.

Slowness is radical.

We don't optimise the walk. There's no step count, no pace goal. The slowness is intentional — it's what makes space for real connection.

Small is a feature.

Every group has a maximum. Not because we want exclusivity, but because intimacy requires it. You can't truly connect with 40 people on a Sunday morning.

Community over content.

We are not building an Instagram aesthetic. We are building friendships. What you experience in person will always matter more than what gets posted.

Curation is care.

We review every application personally. This isn't gatekeeping — it's an act of care for every woman who shows up. We want the room to feel right.

Cities, not feeds.

Girls Who Walk grows city by city, not through viral moments. Each city gets its own rhythm, its own regular spots, its own community.

Presence over performance.

You don't need to arrive ready to network. You don't need a pitch. Come exactly as you are on a Sunday morning.

How We Grow

Slow, deliberate, rooted.

We are not trying to become a platform with millions of users. We are trying to build something that still feels meaningful in three years.

New cities open when there's a woman on the ground who understands the vision and has the network to do it right. No rapid scaling. No franchise model. Just trust.

If you're in a city that doesn't have Girls Who Walk yet, tell us on your waitlist application. You might be the one who starts it.
